# Attribute Systems — the umbrella + models to research

## The search terms that unlock everything

- **Character attributes** / **stats** / **ability scores** — the direct term for mechanics like
  D&D's six.
- **RPG attribute systems** — the genre. This is the decades-long competition between rival ways
  of reducing a "person" to numbers.
- **Game systems design** / **mechanics design** — one level up, the design discipline.
- **Gamification** + **quantified self** — sideways, the non-fiction cousins: applying game
  mechanics to real behavior (which is exactly the Life RPG move).

## Three schools of attribute design

1. **Class-based** — pick an archetype that *bundles* abilities (D&D).
2. **Skill-based** — buy individual competencies directly, no archetype.
3. **Point-buy** — a budget of points, allocate however you want.

Each represents "a person" differently. For a *trainable-traits* system, skill-based and
point-buy are more interesting than class-based, because growth = reallocating points, not
switching archetypes.

## Alternative models worth studying

| System | Model | Why it's relevant here |
|---|---|---|
| **GURPS** | 4 attributes (ST, DX, IQ, HT) + point-buy advantages/disadvantages | Extreme simplicity — proves you don't need 6+ stats. The advantages/disadvantages layer is a nice model for quirks/traits. |
| **BRP / Call of Cthulhu** (d100) | Percentile stats incl. a **Sanity** stat | Directly relevant to the *neuroticism / low-neuroticism* trait — a working model of mental state as a tracked number. |
| **FATE** | Descriptive "Aspects" instead of numbers | The narrative extreme — traits as phrases, not scores. Good counterpoint to over-quantifying. |
| **Powered by the Apocalypse (PbtA)** | Stats = *approaches to action* | Reframes stats as how you act, not what you have — closer to behavior than capacity. |
| **World of Darkness** | 9 attributes: Physical / Social / Mental triads | Cleaner taxonomy than D&D. The triad structure maps more naturally onto personality dimensions. |

## One level up: design theory

- **MDA framework** — Mechanics → Dynamics → Aesthetics. Explains *why* a stat (Mechanic) like
  Charisma produces certain player behaviors (Dynamics) and feelings (Aesthetics). Useful for
  predicting what a real-life stat will actually make you do.

## The crosswalk question (the actual research goal)

> Which RPG attribute taxonomy maps most cleanly onto a **validated** psychological model
> (Big Five)?

Rough first-pass mapping to chew on later:

| Big Five | Closest RPG analog(s) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Openness | INT (D&D) / Mental (WoD) | Curiosity + intellect. |
| Conscientiousness | — (weak in most systems) | RPGs rarely stat this; interesting gap. |
| Extraversion / Assertiveness | CHA (D&D) / Social (WoD) | Social presence + drive. |
| Agreeableness | CHA-adjacent / Social | Warmth vs. influence — often conflated in CHA. |
| Neuroticism (low) | **Sanity** (CoC) / Willpower (WoD) | The one place D&D has *nothing*; other systems do. |

Note the two gaps D&D leaves: **Conscientiousness** and **Neuroticism** have no clean home in
the six ability scores — which is the strongest argument for borrowing from BRP/CoC and World of
Darkness rather than defaulting to D&D.

## Trainable-traits target (from the Life RPG system)

The four (now five) categories the logger tracks map to trait targets, not D&D stats directly:

- **Boundary → Assertiveness**
- **Curiosity → Openness**
- **Care → Agreeableness / Warmth**
- **Energy → Steadiness (low Neuroticism / regulation)**
- **Anchor → somatic regulation** (added most recently)

The open question is whether to keep this bespoke 5-category model or re-derive it from one of
the systems above.
